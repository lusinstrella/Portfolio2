Challenge

The COVID-19 pandemic underscored the necessity for expanded remote teaching and learning opportunities. In fields such as sign language, traditionally reliant on physical presence, advancements in technology now enable us to develop a platform connecting students and instructors in a completely digital space.

Behavioral Archetypes

We focused on adult learners who experienced hearing loss later in life. Our user relies on technology that is sometimes unreliable and expensive.

Research

Understanding the specific needs and expectations of deaf students who lost hearing later in life is crucial for developing a successful remote ASL learning platform.

We conducted in-depth interviews with five diverse individuals

We created a survey and shared it on on four deaf community groups

Key Research Findings

One very important part of the ASL learning experience that we didn’t initially consider is the need for a community of people to practice with, which students reported to be very difficult to find in their day-to-day lives.

58% of the respondents are deaf, while others are looking to learn so they might communicate better with deaf people

50% of respondents indicated that their biggest challenge is limited access to learning options

58% of respondent feel that there are no budget friendly options or engaging features in remote ASL learning Apps

Wireframes

In our initial design as well as in our user’s flow, we focused primarily on the tasks we intended to test. We identified tasks and a specific user flow that would meet our user persona's needs, desires, and goals. These include class enrollment, checking the course overall progress and connecting with tutors

Responsiveness

I coded my website, using some Bootstrap elements and utility classes. In other cases it made more sense to make my own grids and then add a few snippets of media queries. The final result is a dynamic website that is responsive for large screens, desktops, tablets and mobile phones.

GitHub

I published the website on GitHub, which allows me to keep track of all the versions I have made so far. It also makes it very easy to push any future changes

Keeping up with CSS

CSS is a constantly evolving language. New features are being introduced all the time. I like staying on top of it and trying new things. That's way the colors on the websites are defined in the oklch environment, while I use the dynamic viewport for all the main sizing on the site.
